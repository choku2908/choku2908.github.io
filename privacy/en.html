Privacy Policy

Effective: December 1, 2023

In operating the "Sticker Book" service, SSJ (hereinafter referred to as "Company") guides information subjects to the procedures and standards for processing personal information in accordance with Article 30 of the Personal Information Protection Act and promptly and smoothly resolves any complaints related thereto. In order to ensure proper processing, we establish and disclose personal information processing policy as follows.

1. Purpose of Processing Personal Information

The company processes personal information for the following purposes. Personal information being processed will not be used for purposes other than those listed below, and if the purpose of use changes, necessary measures will be taken in accordance with the law, such as obtaining separate consent.

  • It is used for membership registration and management, such as confirmation of intention to become a member, identity verification in accordance with the provision of membership services, user identification, maintenance and management of membership, badge authentication screening, prevention of illegal use, and intention to withdraw membership.
  • It is used to provide services promised to users, to make payments for purchase and use of paid services, to supply goods and services, to handle civil complaints, to deliver notices, and to preserve records in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.
  • It is used for marketing and promotional purposes such as providing event information and participation opportunities, survey information, and advertising information.
  • It is used for customer analysis and management, provision of customized services, service improvement and new service development, and service usage statistics and validation.

2. Personal Information Items Processed

The company processes the minimum amount of personal information necessary to provide various services as follows.

  • For membership registration and management: [Required] Device information
  • To handle complaints and inquiries: [Required] Email address, device information
  • During the process of using the service, the following information may be automatically generated or additionally collected: Device information, IP address, cookies, service usage records, visit records, purchase records

3. Retention and Use Period of Personal Information

  • The company processes and retains personal information within the personal information retention and use period pursuant to laws and regulations, or within the personal information retention and use period agreed upon when collecting personal information from the information subject.
  • All information will be destroyed immediately after withdrawal.

Information that needs to be preserved in accordance with relevant laws and regulations is retained as follows:

  • Records related to display and advertising: 6 months
  • Records of contract or subscription cancellation, payment, supply of goods, etc.: 5 years
  • Records of consumer complaints or dispute resolution: 3 years

4. Provision of Personal Information to Third Parties

  • The company processes the personal information of the information subject only within the scope specified in Section 1, and provides personal information to third parties only with the consent of the information subject or when there are special provisions of the law.
  • In accordance with the "Personal Information Processing and Protection Rules in Emergency Situations" jointly announced by relevant government ministries, personal information may be provided to relevant organizations without the consent of the information subject in emergency situations such as disasters, infectious diseases, or incidents causing imminent life or physical danger.

5. Procedures and Methods for Destroying Personal Information

  • When personal information becomes unnecessary, the company destroys the relevant personal information without delay.
  • In cases where personal information must continue to be preserved pursuant to other laws, the personal information shall be transferred to a separate database (DB) or stored differently.
  • Destruction Procedure: The company selects the personal information that requires destruction and destroys the personal information with the approval of the company's personal information manager.
  • Destruction Method: Electronic files are destroyed so that records cannot be reproduced, and paper documents are destroyed by shredding or incineration.

6. Measures Regarding Destruction of Personal Information of Non-Users

  • The company converts users who have not used the service for one year to dormant accounts and stores personal information separately.
  • The company notifies members scheduled to become dormant at least 30 days prior to the transition to dormancy.
  • If you do not want to convert to a dormant account, please log in to the service before converting. Even if converted, logging in can restore the dormant account.

7. Rights and Obligations of the Information Subject

  • Information subjects can exercise their rights to view, correct, delete, or suspend processing of personal information at any time.
  • Requests to view and suspend processing of personal information may limit the information subject's rights under the Personal Information Protection Act.
  • Personal information can be viewed and corrected at 'My page > Edit profile', and membership withdrawal is possible at 'My page > Settings > Profile management > Withdrawal of membership'.
  • We confirm whether the person making the request is the person or a legitimate agent.

8. Measures to Ensure the Stability of Personal Information

  • Management measures: Establishment and implementation of internal management plan, operation of dedicated organization, regular employee training
  • Technical measures: Management of access rights to personal information processing systems, installation of access control systems, encryption of personal information, installation and update of security programs
  • Physical measures: Access control to computer rooms, data storage rooms, etc.

9. Automatic Collection of Personal Information (Cookies)

  • The company uses 'cookies' to store usage information and retrieve it from time to time in order to provide individualized services to users.
  • Purpose of use of cookies: They are used to provide optimized information to users by identifying visitation and usage patterns, popular search terms, secure access, etc.
  • Installation, operation and refusal of cookies: You can refuse the storage of cookies through option settings in the Tools > Internet Options > Personal Information menu at the top of your web browser.
  • If you refuse to store cookies, you may have difficulty using customized services.

10. Collection, Use and Refusal of Behavioral Information

The company collects and uses behavioral information in the process of using the service to provide optimized customized services and benefits, online customized advertisements, etc.

Items Collected Collection Method Purpose Retention Period
User's app usage history, purchase history Automatically collected when the user visits/runs the app Providing customized advertising and services based on user interests and tendencies Destroyed immediately upon withdrawal of membership

  • The company collects only the minimum behavioral information necessary for online customized advertising.
  • The company does not collect behavioral information for the purpose of customized advertising from children known to be under the age of 14.

11. Additional Use/Provision Judgment Criteria

  • Whether additional use and provision of personal information is related to the original purpose of collection
  • Whether there is a possibility of further use and provision in light of the circumstances in which personal information was collected or processing practices
  • Whether additional use and provision unfairly infringes on the interests of the information subject
  • Whether necessary measures were taken to ensure safety, such as pseudonymization or encryption

12. Remedy for Infringement of Rights

In order to receive relief from personal information infringement, the information subject may apply for dispute resolution or consultation to the following organizations:

  • Personal Information Dispute Mediation Committee: 1833-6972 (www.kopico.go.kr)
  • Personal Information Infringement Reporting Center: 118 (privacy.kisa.or.kr)
  • Supreme Prosecutors' Office: 1301 (www.spo.go.kr)
  • National Police Agency: 182 (ecrm.cyber.go.kr)

13. Changes to Personal Information Processing Policy

This personal information processing policy is effective from December 1, 2023 onwards.
